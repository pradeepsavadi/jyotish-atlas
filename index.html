The core idea

Three alphabets, one grammar

Almost everything in a horoscope is generated from three small sets and a few "states." You don't memorize meanings — you build them.

9
Grahas
The actors — what happens (soul, mind, drive, wisdom…)
12
Rashis
The costumes — how it expresses (the sign's flavor)
12
Bhavas
The stages — where in life it plays out (houses)
+
States
Dignity, varga & aspect — how strong & whether it delivers

9 × 12 × 12 = 1,296 base placements — each shifting again with dignity, divisional charts and aspect. You can't memorize that. You derive it. Try the Synthesizer.

Jaimini · Chara Kārakas, Kārakāṁśa & Argala

The Chara Kārakas are movable significators: the planet at the highest degree in its sign becomes the Ātmakāraka (soul significator), and the rest fall in order of descending degree.

Planetary Avasthas · states of a planet

A planet can be well-placed yet unable to deliver. Bālādi reads its life-stage by degree (peaks at Yuvā), Jāgradādi its wakefulness, Dīptādi its condition. Effective strength leads with dignity across D-1, D-9 & D-10 plus vargottama — the primary strength — and only modulates by avastha, so an exalted, own-sign or vargottama planet stays strong even at a weak degree. The notes flag where promise (D-1) and delivery (D-9 / D-10) diverge.

Jaimini · Planetary Reflections

Graha Arudhas

Each planet has a Graha Arudha — the material, external reflection of that planet's archetype. Since most planets rule two signs, they each cast two Arudhas. The rule (BPHS Ch.32): count how many signs the planet's owned sign is from the planet itself, then count that same number forward from the planet. Where this reflection lands is where that planetary energy — solar authority, lunar emotion, Martian drive — manifests as a visible, tangible force in your world. The final cluster analysis shows which houses receive the most reflections, revealing the dominant material arenas of your life.
